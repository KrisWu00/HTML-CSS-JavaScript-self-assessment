The answer to the fifth question

  • 'px' units are useful when you want font sizes to maintain precise control over their size.
  • 'em' units are useful when you want font sizes to scale consistently based on the parent element's font size.
  • Percentage units are useful when you want font sizes to be relative to the parent element's font size.
  • 'vw' units are useful when you want font sizes to adjust according to the width of the viewport.
  • 'vh' units are useful when you want font sizes to adapt to the height of the viewport.
  • 'rem' units are useful when you want font sizes to remain consistent across your entire website while being based on the root element's size.
